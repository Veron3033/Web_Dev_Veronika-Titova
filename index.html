"Flow"

Flow (2024) is an animated feature film created by Latvian filmmaker Gints Zilbalodis. The film tells the story of a cat traveling through a flooded world, forming bonds with other animals to survive. The movie is known for its wordless storytelling, stunning visuals, and emotional depth. It explores themes of coexistence, resilience, and adaptation through a visually poetic narrative.

Flow received a lot of accolades. Here are some of the major accolades that Flow has received:

  • Academy Awards (Oscars)
  • Golden Globe Awards
  • LUX Audience Award
  • Lielais Kristaps
  • Athens International Film Festival

About author

Gints Zilbalodis (born 13 April 1994) is a Latvian filmmaker, animator and composer. A prominent figure of Latvian animation, Zilbalodis's films are often minimalist, explore themes of journey, isolation, survival against nature, and the power of collaboration and camaraderie. Zilbalodis is best known for his works of animated adventure films Away (2019) and Flow (2024).
